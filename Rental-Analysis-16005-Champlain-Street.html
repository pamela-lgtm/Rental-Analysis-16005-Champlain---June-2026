How the subject stacks up

Here's the honest picture of where 16005 Champlain sits in the comp set:

Strengths (+)

  • Show-ready and ready to lease immediately — no friction during showings
  • 2017 build — three years newer than St Clair (the closest size match) and inside the active-builder-community age band
  • 2,585 sf — solid family size, essentially identical to the strongest 4/2.5 size comp (St Clair at 2,587 sf)
  • 2-car garage — standard expectation for this market
  • Late spring / early summer is peak rental season in greater Orlando — relocations and family moves drive demand now

Considerations (−)

  • 4/2.5 configuration — two full baths plus a powder, which is the value tier in this submarket vs. the 4/3.5 standard
  • The half-bath difference vs. 4/3.5 homes is worth roughly $200–$300/month in this comp set — an unavoidable structural ceiling
  • Clermont submarket is rental-heavy with active builder competition — pricing discipline matters more than aspirational pricing

The 21-day playbook

Pricing & marketing protocol

1

Launch at $2,750 with professional photos, polished marketing copy, and full Verandah pipeline activation. Goal: capture serious renter interest in the first 7–10 days while the listing is fresh.

2

Day 10 checkpoint. Review showing activity, applicant feedback, and pipeline strength. Low-traffic listings tell us something is off; strong traffic with no applications tells us a different story. Either way, day 10 is the first decision point.

3

Day 17–21 decision point. If no approved application is in pipeline, the data-supported next move is a reduction to $2,695 or $2,650 to slot back into the fastest-leasing 4/2.5 band. The drop is small ($55–$100/mo) but signals motivation and typically pulls the next motivated renter quickly.

4

Beyond day 30 — honest conversation. If the property is still without an application, we revisit the listing itself: photos, copy, showing experience, and any feedback. Going below $2,650 should be a last resort, since the data shows that band consistently leases when presented well.
